William Shakespeare

Love is a theme that is explored in literature across different time periods and genres, with varying interpretations of its nature and effects. William Shakespeare in his sonnet 116 “Let me not to the Marriage of True Minds” describes love as eternal and unchanging, enduring even in the face of time and hardship. In contrast, William Faulkner in his short story “A Rose for Emily” explores love through the life of Emily Grierson. He shows the dark side of love and says love is decaying, destructive and obsessive. Shakespeare idealizes love as an unbreakable force that transcends time, Faulkner challenges this notion by portraying love as something that worsens when entangled with obsession and dear of change. While both texts explore love and time, Faulkner presents love as destructive, distorted by control and decaying whereas Shakespeare idealizes love as unwavering and eternal.
